How might we equipt educators of at-risk youth?

A Design Thinking approach exploring how we can give teachers the tools to better prepare kids for a successful future, especially the ones that need guidance most.

Challenge

During the summer of 2015, I took an 8 week long design thinking course. The class partnered with PBS to come up with solutions for how the studio may better leverage its network and resources to better equipt and empower educators of at-risk youth to make a difference in their students' lives. The solution needed to be inclusive to everyone, take advantage of PBS' already existing resources, and invite collaboration between PBS partners. The ideal solution was expected to reduce structural and systematic gaps at-risk youth often face in access to resources and pursuit of opportunities while fulfilling PBS' mission to be a public service provider to the nation.

Insights

During the first stage of the design process, we interviewed students who, during their high school years, were in some ways at a disadvantage compared to their peers when it came to factors such as financials and at-home life. We also talked to teachers who had worked with students like this. We discovered that all students value positive role models and greatly benefit from some kind of mentorship in their lives. Teachers told us that students feel more confident in their ability to succeed when they can get advice and guidance from someone who they relate to. We then developed the criteria our solution would need to meet based on these uncovered insights.
